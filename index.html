Method

The dataset starts from the latest global IUCN Red List assessments. We keep the most granular endangered taxonomy level available (species or subspecies).

Spatial placement uses IUCN and BirdLife range data. The representative geometry is chosen from a prioritized combination of presence (Extant, Probably Extant, Possibly Extant) and seasonality (Resident, Breeding, Non-breeding, Passage). Centroids are then computed to get one point per species. Species with disconnected ranges get displayed on their largest distribution area. Species with disconnected ranges can receive several label points (max 2), but secondary clusters are kept only when they represent enough of the total mapped range (min 20%).

Name size and weight come from Wikipedia pageviews over the last completed year (Jun 25 – May 26). More-viewed animals appear earlier; zooming in reveals less-viewed species. Photos come from Wikidata first, then Wikipedia and Wikimedia Commons fallbacks, and finally iNaturalist when available.

Spatial data: IUCN 2025. The IUCN Red List of Threatened Species. Version 2025-2. https://www.iucnredlist.org. Downloaded on 14 June 2026. Data last updated 10 October 2025.
